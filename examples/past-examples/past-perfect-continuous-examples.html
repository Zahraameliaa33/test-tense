Past Perfect Continuous Examples

  • (+) I had been studying for two hours before dinner.
  • (-) I had not been studying for two hours before dinner.
  • (?) Had I been studying for two hours before dinner?
  • (+) You had been waiting for the bus when it finally arrived.
  • (-) You had not been waiting for the bus when it finally arrived.
  • (?) Had you been waiting for the bus when it finally arrived?
  • (+) They had been playing football for an hour before it started raining.
  • (-) They had not been playing football for an hour before it started raining.
  • (?) Had they been playing football for an hour before it started raining?
  • (+) We had been traveling all morning before we reached the city.
  • (-) We had not been traveling all morning before we reached the city.
  • (?) Had we been traveling all morning before we reached the city?
  • (+) She had been working at the company for five years before she got promoted.
  • (-) She had not been working at the company for five years before she got promoted.
  • (?) Had she been working at the company for five years before she got promoted?
  • (+) He had been reading the book for hours before he fell asleep.
  • (-) He had not been reading the book for hours before he fell asleep.
  • (?) Had he been reading the book for hours before he fell asleep?
  • (+) It had been raining for two hours before the sun came out.
  • (-) It had not been raining for two hours before the sun came out.
  • (?) Had it been raining for two hours before the sun came out?
