Present Perfect Continuous Examples

  • (+) I have been studying for two hours.
  • (-) I have not been studying for two hours.
  • (?) Have I been studying for two hours?
  • (+) You have been working all day.
  • (-) You have not been working all day.
  • (?) Have you been working all day?
  • (+) They have been playing football since morning.
  • (-) They have not been playing football since morning.
  • (?) Have they been playing football since morning?
  • (+) We have been waiting for the bus for 30 minutes.
  • (-) We have not been waiting for the bus for 30 minutes.
  • (?) Have we been waiting for the bus for 30 minutes?
  • (+) She has been reading that book all week.
  • (-) She has not been reading that book all week.
  • (?) Has she been reading that book all week?
  • (+) He has been working on his project since morning.
  • (-) He has not been working on his project since morning.
  • (?) Has he been working on his project since morning?
  • (+) It has been raining since early morning.
  • (-) It has not been raining since early morning.
  • (?) Has it been raining since early morning?
