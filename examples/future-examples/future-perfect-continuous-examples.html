Future Perfect Continuous Examples

  • (+) I will have been studying for 3 hours by 6 PM.
  • (-) I will not have been studying for 3 hours by 6 PM.
  • (?) Will I have been studying for 3 hours by 6 PM?
  • (+) You will have been working on the project for 5 days by Friday.
  • (-) You will not have been working on the project for 5 days by Friday.
  • (?) Will you have been working on the project for 5 days by Friday?
  • (+) They will have been playing football for 2 hours by noon.
  • (-) They will not have been playing football for 2 hours by noon.
  • (?) Will they have been playing football for 2 hours by noon?
  • (+) We will have been traveling for 10 hours by midnight.
  • (-) We will not have been traveling for 10 hours by midnight.
  • (?) Will we have been traveling for 10 hours by midnight?
  • (+) She will have been reading for 2 hours by 8 PM.
  • (-) She will not have been reading for 2 hours by 8 PM.
  • (?) Will she have been reading for 2 hours by 8 PM?
  • (+) He will have been practicing the guitar for 4 hours by dinner time.
  • (-) He will not have been practicing the guitar for 4 hours by dinner time.
  • (?) Will he have been practicing the guitar for 4 hours by dinner time?
  • (+) It will have been raining for 6 hours by midnight.
  • (-) It will not have been raining for 6 hours by midnight.
  • (?) Will it have been raining for 6 hours by midnight?
